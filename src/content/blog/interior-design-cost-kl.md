## Interior Design Fees vs Renovation Costs — What's the Difference?

First, an important distinction that many homeowners miss.

**Interior design fees** are what you pay the designer for their professional services — space planning, 3D concept drawings, material selection, contractor coordination, and project management.

**Renovation costs** are what you pay the contractors for the actual physical work — carpentry, tiling, electrical, plumbing, painting, and so on.

These are two separate budgets. When you hire a full-service studio like Wuwa, both are covered under one roof — but you should understand what each component costs.

## Interior Design Fees in KL (2025)

Design fees in Malaysia are typically structured in one of two ways:

**Fixed project fee** — A lump sum covering the full scope of design services. For residential projects in Klang Valley, this typically starts from RM5,000 for a smaller scope and scales up depending on property size and complexity.

**Percentage of renovation cost** — Some studios charge 10–15% of your total renovation budget as their design fee.

## Renovation Budgets by Property Type

Here's a realistic guide to renovation costs for common property types in Klang Valley:

**Condominium (800–1,200 sq ft)**
A partial renovation — kitchen cabinet, wardrobes, feature wall, flooring — typically runs between RM30,000 to RM60,000. A full renovation including bathrooms and hacking can reach RM80,000 and above depending on finishes.

**Terrace House (1,500–2,000 sq ft)**
A standard full renovation for a double-storey terrace in Shah Alam or Puchong — covering all rooms, kitchen, bathrooms, and built-in carpentry — typically ranges from RM80,000 to RM150,000.

**Landed Home / Semi-D / Bungalow**
Larger scope projects start from RM150,000 and can go significantly higher depending on the level of finish and structural changes involved.

## What Affects the Final Cost?

Several factors will push your budget up or down:

**Finish level** — Laminate vs solid wood, porcelain vs marble, local vs imported fittings. The material choices you make account for a significant portion of your budget.

**Scope of work** — Are you refreshing just the living and dining area, or doing a full gut renovation including bathrooms? The wider the scope, the higher the cost.

**Property condition** — Sub-sale properties often require more hacking and remedial work than new hand-over units.

**Timeline** — Rush jobs cost more. A realistic timeline of 12–20 weeks allows for proper planning and competitive contractor pricing.
